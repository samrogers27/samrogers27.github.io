So What's the Deal?

LaCroix's current packaging is busy, outdated, and doesn't stand out among competitors. The current design was also chosen before the company decided to market primarily to millennials and is disconnected with the lifestyle they promote.

Let's Fix It.

The new packaging and identity should fit the modern, bright, positive lifestyle that LaCroix advertises, as well as break through the flat, homogenous look of the supermarket shelves.

A bright and attractive, but reduced, color palette leads to a more recognizable and cohesive brand while bold, modern graphics appeal to younger buyers. The use of a water-inspired graphic ties LaCroix to its product, as well as its namesake, Wisconsin's St. Croix River, to subtly ground the brand in its own history.

The use of glass bottles and an open carrying case allows customers to see what they are buying and gives texture to the towering wall of enclosed sparkling water boxes on store shelves.
