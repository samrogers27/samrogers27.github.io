Cal Poly Racing

Creative Direction / Identity Design

While at Cal Poly, I did design work as a member of Cal Poly Racing. Eventually I was promoted to Creative Director where I had the opportunity to recruit, train, and lead a creative team.

Here are some of my favorite projects.

Website

I worked with a team of 3 other designers—Spencer White, Alina Chiu, and Runar Schmidt—to create Cal Poly Racing's new website. As Creative Director, I handled establishing a branded UI, building the website, and collaborating with the engineering teams to write copy.

The goal of the redesign was to present accurate information in an approachable format so that prospective members and partners can learn more about the team.

Brand Standards

There are hundreds of SAE teams all over the world and Cal Poly Racing needed a brand that stands out and reflects the values of its members.

I established a recognizable visual language that highlights a dedicated, professional, and exciting team culture—then wrapped it up into a standards manual so that it can be used long after I've graduated.

Apparel

Team apparel needs to represent Formula SAE and Baja SAE, but just putting pictures of the cars on a shirt is an extremely common look among college racing teams.

Instead I broke the cars down into some of their most recognizable parts. Not only does this represent both teams overall, it also recognizes the work of the subsystems within each team that design and build individual components.

2019 Baja Car Livery

For the 2019 season I worked with the Baja team to design livery for the side body panels and front and back of the firewall.

I created graphics that reflect the dynamic and rugged nature of off-roading while still adhering to the parameters set by the team. The design was still clearly visible through sponsor logos and tech inspection stickers and allowed the car to be identified from a distance during competitions.

Promotional Material

Even smaller pieces are important elements of the team’s presence on campus and in the auto industry and further our credibility as a professional organization.
